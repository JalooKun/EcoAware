Understanding the Crisis

What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While natural causes like volcanic eruptions and solar cycles have contributed, human activities — especially the burning of fossil fuels — have been the main driver since the 1800s.

The Earth's average temperature has risen by about 1.1°C since the pre-industrial era. This may seem small, but it has enormous consequences: more extreme weather events, melting ice caps, rising sea levels, and threats to biodiversity.

Causes of Climate Change

Global Emissions Overview

Since 1950, global CO₂ emissions have increased by over 400%. The five sectors on the right are the largest contributors to global greenhouse gas emissions today.

Source: Our World in Data — CO₂ & Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Energy

Burning coal, oil, and gas for electricity and heat is the single largest source of global emissions.

Industrial Pollution

Factories producing cement, steel, and chemicals release massive CO₂ and other potent greenhouse gases.

Agriculture, Forestry & Land Use

Livestock, fertilizers, and deforestation reduce carbon sinks and emit methane and nitrous oxide.

Transport

Cars, trucks, ships, and planes burning fossil fuels account for nearly a quarter of global CO₂ emissions.

Buildings

Heating, cooling, lighting, and construction of homes and offices consume huge amounts of energy.
